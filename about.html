My Journey

The PROCESS

At the beginning of my developer journey, I spent my days learning my first programming language and my nights working at a restaurant. After months of learning on my own with free prep courses, I earnestly applied to Hack Reactor Galvanize and took their technical assessment, but I failed… twice. I felt so disappointed; filled with insecurities that I wasn’t smart enough to code, but I realized that the path of self-learning required momentum, commitment and discipline. Between my current job and the process of changing careers, something had to give if I truly wanted to upgrade my life and become a developer. So, I gave my 2-weeks resignation to study full time. Months after, I applied to University of Denver’s Full Stack Flex Developer Program and scored 100% on the technical aptitude assessment.

Like an ember, acing that assessment was the spark I needed, without which I may have never come to love software development, receive a certificate for ‘Best UX/UI Design’ or proudly present my projects in front of major companies at DU Demo Day. For that, I am truly grateful for the opportunities that this program and the educational staff has provided. As a DU graduate coder, I gained the discipline to learn and work independently and virtually, fully remote due to the COVID-19 pandemic. With every project, the days were long; twelve to fourteen hours group Zoom calls spent refactoring the back end, finessing the front end and problem solving with classmates for hours. We were taught humility when the professor had the solution in a matter of minutes and resilience when all we had to help us out was Stack Overflow. I was toiling away for endless nights, more often because urgency and deadlines took precedence, but it only sustained my passion for coding even more.

For the better part of the past year, I was trying desperately to stay in love with the process, trust it, though everything seemed to pull me away from it. This process allowed me to reflect on my own transformation and dig deep into the reasons why I strive to be a changing force in software development, for women in technology and for the brave individuals willing to step into the ever-changing world of software development.

I welcome you to explore my portfolio and my past works, and reach out if you're willing to take a chance on an unknown developer.

Values

Values

1 - Creating Polished Content

No matter what the topic, idea or project is, it's the execution that matters most. What the audience sees makes a huge impression and it's an important factor in the success of a business. Front end development and UX design are my strengths and I aim to make the client's vision come to life.

2 - Delivering Genuine Value

Development time correlates with price and by writing correct and efficient code shortens the development time. I believe value is determined by the client and what matters most to clients are the services that exactly meet their most important needs within their budget in respect to the time and work I contribute to build a wonderful application.

3 - Continuous Improvement

These values align because creating a seamless application requires mapping out efficient processes, writing clean and well developed code/functions, and an incessant effort for continuous improvement.
